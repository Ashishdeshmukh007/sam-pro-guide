The one-line story of SAM

A company buys tickets (licenses) and people take seats (installations/usage). SAM continuously compares tickets vs seats. Too few tickets = compliance risk (audit penalties). Too many tickets = wasted money. SAM Pro is the machine that counts both sides automatically, and every chapter in this guide is just a deeper look at that machine.

SAM Fundamentals: the job before the tool

SAM Pro is just a machine. This part teaches what the machine is for. Master these ideas and every screen in the tool will make sense.

1.1 What Software Asset Management actually is

⚖️Stay compliant

Prove you own the right to every install. Fail this and a publisher audit turns into a multi-million dollar invoice.

💰Stop waste

Find licenses nobody uses and shelfware nobody deploys. Gartner estimates up to ~30% of software spend can be optimized.

🔭See everything

One trusted inventory of what is installed, used, and owned, across datacenter, desktop, SaaS and cloud.

🤝Negotiate smarter

Walk into every renewal knowing exactly what you consume. Data beats vendor sales pressure.

📖
Analogy: the parking garage

Think of a parking garage. Entitlements are the parking passes you bought. Installations are cars actually parked. The SAM team walks the floors counting cars, checks the pass ledger, and reports: floor 2 has 40 cars but only 30 passes (buy 10 or tow 10), floor 5 has 100 passes and 20 cars (stop buying passes). That walk-the-floors-and-compare loop is the entire profession. SAM Pro automates the walking and the counting.

The cost iceberg (why the license price is the smallest number)

💵 License purchase pricethe visible tip everyone negotiates
🔧 Support & maintenance, 20–22% every yearexceeds the license cost after ~5 years
📦 Waste: shelfware, unused seats, oversized tierstypically 25–30% of spend
⚖️ Audit exposure: back-licenses + back-support at list pricethe part that sinks ships

SAM is the only discipline that works all four layers at once. Procurement sees layer 1; auditors monetize layer 4.

Negative position = compliance gap (buy, reclaim, or re-architect). Positive position = savings opportunity (reharvest, reduce renewal).

"Effective" matters: the position is only real after applying license rules like upgrade rights, downgrade rights, and product use rights. 1.7 License types and rights

🏛️Perpetual

Pay once, use forever (that version). Optional annual maintenance/support ~20-22%.

📅Subscription

Pay per term (month/year). Stop paying = stop using. Now the dominant model.

☁️SaaS

Subscription where the vendor also runs the software. Counted in seats/consumption, discovered via APIs not installs.

🆓Freemium / OSS

"Free" with paid tiers or support. Java and Docker Desktop proved free can become chargeable.

Where licenses come from: the channel taxonomy

🏭OEM

Pre-installed with hardware (Windows on a laptop). Dies with the device; never transferable to another machine. Cheap but rigid.

📦Retail / FPP

Boxed or single online purchase. Transferable but expensive; rare in enterprises except emergencies.

🏢Volume licensing

Enterprise agreements and programs (EA, MPSA, Passport Advantage). Discounts, downgrade rights, central records. The SAM heartland.

🔁Subscription / cloud

Per-seat or consumption via CSP, marketplaces, direct portals. Flexible but auto-renewing; waste hides here.

🛒Cloud marketplace

Bought inside AWS/Azure/GCP bills, often by engineers. Frequently invisible to procurement until spend detection finds it.

🆓Open source

"Free" with obligations (GPL copyleft, attribution) and commercial traps: Oracle JDK, Docker Desktop, Redis/Elastic license changes. Inventory it like paid software.

⚠️
Channel determines rights

The same product bought OEM vs volume has different transfer, downgrade and virtualization rights. During entitlement collection, always record the channel; an auditor will.

Anatomy of a license agreement (learn to read the six clauses that matter)

✍️1 · Grant & scope

What you may do (install, run, copy for backup) and for which legal entities, territories and purposes. Affiliates and outsourcers are often NOT covered unless named.

📐2 · Metric definition

The exact counting unit and its definitions ("Named User Plus means…"). In a dispute, this paragraph beats every blog post and every sales email.

⏳3 · Term & termination

Perpetual vs term, renewal mechanics, what survives termination (perpetual rights? data retrieval periods for SaaS?).

🔁4 · Assignment & transfer

Can licenses move to an acquirer, a divested entity, a new datacenter, a cloud? M&A deals quietly die on this clause.

🔍5 · Audit / verification clause

Notice period, frequency limits, who may audit, confidentiality, cost allocation. Negotiate this BEFORE signing; it is your future battlefield's map.

📎6 · Order of precedence

Which document wins when they conflict: ordering document → program terms → master agreement → (never) policy PDFs. Memorize the hierarchy per publisher.

💡
Consultant habit

For every Tier-1 publisher at a client, keep a one-page "contract facts" sheet: metric definitions, audit clause terms, assignment rights, special concessions. Whoever brings that sheet to the audit kickoff controls the meeting.

2.1 Why SAM on ServiceNow at all?

Point SAM tools (Flexera, Snow) are deep counters. ServiceNow's pitch is different: SAM living on the same platform as your CMDB, service catalog, workflows and ITSM data.

🗄️One CMDB, one truth

SAM reads the same configuration items (servers, VMs, clusters) that ITSM and Discovery maintain. No nightly sync between a SAM tool's inventory and "the real" inventory.

🔁Workflows built in

Reclamation, approvals, software requests and true-up tasks run as native ServiceNow workflows with notifications and SLAs.

🧩Suite gravity

HAM (hardware), SaaS management, Cloud insights and App portfolio all share the data model. One skill set, many modules.

🎤
Classic interview question: "SAM Pro vs Flexera/Snow?"

Balanced answer: point tools historically had deeper discovery/inventory tech and older publisher content; SAM Pro wins on platform integration (CMDB, catalog, workflow), operational automation, and total cost when ServiceNow is already the ITSM backbone. In practice SAM Pro often consumes inventory from SCCM/ILMT/Jamf anyway, so the battle is content + workflow, not agents. Never trash-talk either tool; consultants meet both.

3.6 Inside the reconciliation engine

  1. Scope selection
    Runs per publisher (or all), on schedule (typically nightly/weekly) or on demand after data changes.
  2. Gather consumption
    Pull normalized installs, subscriptions and usage; join hardware facts (cores, sockets, virtualization topology) from the CMDB for infrastructure metrics.
  3. Apply license metrics
    Each software model + entitlement pair has a metric (per user, per core, PVU…). Metric calculators convert deployment into consumed rights, respecting minimums and core factors.
  4. Apply product use rights
    Downgrade/upgrade rights let newer entitlements cover older versions (and vice versa with maintenance). Suite logic and license mobility applied here too.
  5. Allocate entitlements
    The engine assigns owned rights to consumption optimally (e.g. burn version-locked licenses before flexible ones), producing per-model results.
  6. Publish results
    Reconciliation results per software model: rights owned, rights consumed, compliance status, potential true-up cost, plus remediation options for shortfalls and reclamation candidates for surpluses.
🧪
Try it

After any entitlement change: Software Asset ▸ Reconciliation ▸ Run reconciliation and pick the publisher. Never present numbers from a stale run; check the "last reconciled" timestamp first. Auditors and interviewers both ask "how fresh is this position?"

3.7 License Workbench: validate before you publish

🔬What it does

Per publisher/product, shows how the engine reached the position: which entitlements were applied, with which rights, against which installs. Drill from summary number to individual install.

🛡️Why it matters

This is your audit-defense and QA surface. Before showing an ELP to a customer (or an auditor), validate the allocation logic here and adjust models/entitlements until the story is defensible.

🩺Debug pattern

Position looks wrong? Check in order: normalization status → software model mapping → metric on the entitlement → hardware attributes on the CI → use rights flags (SA/S&S). 90% of "engine bugs" are data bugs.

3.9 Under the hood: how a raw install actually gets matched

Interviewers separate juniors from seniors with one question: "what exactly happens between discovery and a normalized model?" Here is the matching ladder the platform walks for every discovery model, top to bottom, first hit wins:

  1. Exact library match
    Publisher + product + version + edition string matches a Content Library entry directly (best case: the installer wrote clean registry data or a SWID tag exists). Status: Normalized.
  2. Pattern / partial match
    The library recognizes the product but must parse version or edition out of messy strings ("v11.2 ENT x64"). Whatever cannot be parsed stays blank. Status: Partially normalized or Publisher normalized.
  3. Customer normalization rules
    Your manual mappings (created from the discovery model form or in bulk) outrank future library updates, so a fix stays fixed. Status: Manually normalized. Rule of thumb: write rules generic enough to survive version churn ("contains 'AcmeApp'" rather than one exact build string).
  4. No match
    Status New: invisible to reconciliation until you map it or the library learns it (you can submit titles to ServiceNow's content team).
💡
Edition inference: the silent money-mover

When edition cannot be determined, some flows let you default it on the software model (e.g. treat unknown SQL as Standard, not Enterprise). That single assumption can swing an ELP by millions, so whatever you choose: write it in the assumptions log and be consistent between audit defense (conservative) and savings hunting (aggressive).

4.10 The deliverable: building an ELP in SAM Pro

"Build me an ELP for publisher X" is the defining request of this job. Inside the tool, an ELP is not one button; it is a quality-gated assembly line:

  1. Freeze scope & check data freshness
    Publisher, products, legal entities, environments in scope. Verify every inventory source imported recently and discovery coverage >95% for the relevant estate. Record the data cut date; every ELP is "as of" a date.
  2. Pass the normalization gate
    Coverage >85–90% for the publisher's discovery models. Fix the top un-normalized models by install count before proceeding; the engine ignores what it cannot recognize.
  3. Verify hardware & topology for infra products
    Cores, sockets, vCPUs and cluster/host links present on the CIs behind SQL, Oracle DB, WebSphere etc. Missing attributes = silently wrong core/PVU math.
  4. Load & validate entitlements
    MLS / PPA / import sets in; spot-check the ten biggest lines against contracts: metric, quantity, SA/S&S dates, restrictions. Attach evidence references.
  5. Run reconciliation for the publisher
    On demand, after the data work, not before. Note the run timestamp.
  6. Validate in License Workbench
    Trace allocation on the big-ticket products: right entitlements consumed, use rights applied sensibly, no surprise unlicensed piles caused by mapping errors. Iterate data → re-run until the story holds.
  7. Publish the position pack
    Compliance summary per product (owned / consumed / position / true-up estimate), remediation options for gaps, reclamation candidates for surpluses, assumptions & data-cut notes, sign-off from stakeholders. That pack is the ELP.

6.4 Options & packs: the "enabled by default" trap

VMware, Hyper-V and friends. Oracle's policy: the VM could run anywhere, so license everywhere it could run.

7.3 ILMT and its accepted alternatives

🔵ILMT

IBM License Metric Tool: free, BigFix-agent based. Scans servers, detects IBM software, measures VM core consumption over time, produces the official quarterly PVU reports.

🔁Accepted alternatives

BigFix Inventory, Flexera One/FNMS and ServiceNow SAM Pro have been accepted by IBM for sub-capacity reporting under conditions. Verify current acceptance terms in the engagement; put it in writing with IBM.

🩺Health checks that save millions

Agent coverage vs CMDB (every eligible server reporting?), scan freshness, unclassified software ("shared" components mapped to the right product), report generation actually scheduled.

Bundles & "supporting programs": the hidden double-count trap

🎁What IBM ships

Big products bundle limited-use components: WebSphere may include a restricted Db2, a monitoring agent, an MQ client. They install like the real thing and discovery sees them as the real thing.

🧨The failure mode

Auditors (or a naive tool setup) count the bundled Db2 as a full Db2 needing PVU entitlements. Or the reverse: teams use the limited component beyond its permitted purpose, which genuinely requires a full license.

🛡️The fix

In ILMT this is the classification/bundling review: assign detected components to their parent product. In SAM Pro, verify the mapping quarterly. In audits, challenge every standalone claim for software that shipped inside something else.

R4HA: the four-hour heartbeat that IS your bill

MLC products bill on the Rolling 4-Hour Average of MSU consumption: every interval, average the last 4 hours; the highest R4HA of the month (summed across LPARs where the product ran) sets that month's charge. Not installed capacity. Not the raw spike. The rolling average peak.

Raw spikes (thin line) matter less than the smoothed peak (thick line). Cap or move the peak and the bill drops, even if total work is unchanged.

The cost levers (where mainframe SAM earns its fees)

🧢1 · Soft capping

Set defined capacity / group capacity limits so WLM caps the R4HA. You trade burst headroom for a guaranteed ceiling on the bill.

🌙2 · Move the peak

Reschedule batch away from the online peak so the 4-hour averages never stack. Same work, smaller peak, smaller invoice.

🏝️3 · Isolate products

MLC products are charged on the LPARs where they run. Fence Db2 or MQ onto fewer LPARs and their MSU base shrinks.

🧩4 · zIIP offload

Push eligible work (Db2 DRDA, Java, some utilities) to zIIPs; those MSUs vanish from the software meter.

🤝5 · ISV contracts

BMC, Broadcom and Rocket software on z is priced on MIPS/MSU tiers too, and renewals are negotiable. Often the fastest saving on the box.

📉6 · Tune the top jobs

CPU-hungry code at peak hours is a licensing problem. One optimized query at 10 a.m. can outperform months of negotiation.

8.4 The audit-defense playbook (any publisher)

    Run your own measurement in SAM Pro before returning anything. Know your gaps and your defenses (isolation evidence, SA/S&S status, passive servers) in advance.
  4. Remediate what you can, fast
    Uninstall unused options, fix misconfigurations, document dates. Findings fixed before the data cut may leave the negotiation entirely.
  5. Negotiate findings commercially
    Auditor reports are opening positions built on worst-case interpretations (list price, policy readings). Challenge methodology, provide counter-evidence, and convert any settlement into future spend you needed anyway.
  6. Close the loop
    Root-cause every finding into a control: new reclamation rule, architecture standard, request-time check. An audit should make the program stronger.
✅Do

Single point of contact · written scope · own numbers first · involve legal early · keep an evidence archive (reports, isolation configs, entitlement proofs) year-round.

🚫Don't

Run auditor scripts unreviewed · give raw data dumps · admit conclusions in meetings · let deadlines be dictated · treat the auditor's Excel as truth.

10.3 The question bank

Bank A · SAM fundamentals (15)

What is Software Asset Management, and why do companies fund it?BASIC

SAM is the practice of continuously knowing what software you own, what you deploy and use, and keeping the two in a defensible, optimized balance. Companies fund it for three returns: audit risk removal, waste elimination (shelfware, unused seats, often ~30% of spend optimizable), and fact-based renewal negotiation.

Define ELP: Effective License Position.BASIC

Entitlements owned minus license consumption, after applying product use rights (downgrade/upgrade, suites, mobility, failover). "Effective" is the key word: raw install-vs-purchase counts are wrong until the rights math is applied, which is exactly what a reconciliation engine automates.

What is the difference between a license and an entitlement?BASIC

The license is the legal right to use software under conditions; the entitlement is the recorded proof of purchase of those rights (quantity, metric, dates, agreement). In SAM Pro, entitlements are records against a software model that the engine allocates to consumption.

Compliance or cost optimization: which do you tackle first?MID

Sequence by exposure: quantify compliance risk first for audit-active publishers (Oracle, IBM, Microsoft datacenter), because a single finding can exceed years of savings. But ship one savings quick-win (reclamation, M365 right-sizing) in parallel, because savings fund and legitimize the program politically.

Perpetual vs subscription: what changes for SAM?BASIC

Perpetual: rights never expire, maintenance status decides upgrade rights, history matters (5 years of POs). Subscription: rights expire with the term, so date handling and renewal calendars matter more than archaeology, and optimization shifts from "uninstall" to "unassign before renewal".

Name license metrics you have worked with.BASIC

Per user and per device (desktop, CALs), per core with minimums (Microsoft), processor with core factor (Oracle), NUP with minimums (Oracle), PVU full and sub-capacity (IBM), RVU (IBM), VPC (Cloud Paks), concurrent, per employee (Java SE Universal), and SaaS subscription seats. Bonus point: the metric, not the product, determines the data you must collect.

Explain downgrade and upgrade rights.MID

Downgrade: a newer license may cover older versions (standard in volume licensing), so SQL 2022 entitlements can cover 2016 installs. Upgrade: older licenses cover newer versions only with active maintenance (SA for Microsoft, S&S for IBM). Reconciliation must apply both to avoid false shortfalls or hidden gaps.

What is a true-up?BASIC

The periodic (typically annual, in EAs) reporting and payment for deployment growth since the last count. A SAM-run true-up is based on your reconciled numbers; a vendor-run one is based on theirs. That difference is usually worth real money.

What is shelfware and how do you attack it?BASIC

Licenses purchased but never (or no longer) deployed or used. Attack: usage-based reclamation rules, request-time allocation from the existing pool before any purchase, and cutting the quantity at the next renewal with usage evidence.

What is ISO 19770 and why would you mention it?MID

The ITAM standards family: 19770-1 defines process maturity for trustworthy ITAM, 19770-2 defines SWID tags for software identification, 19770-3 an entitlement schema. Mentioning it signals you think in frameworks, and SWID tags are genuinely useful for precise recognition.

What typically triggers a software audit?MID

Money events the vendor can see: declining or cancelled support/maintenance, EA or ULA expiry approaching, M&A activity, big hardware refresh, resisting a cloud migration pitch, and product-specific tells like Oracle Java download logs. Random selection exists but is the minority.

Why does virtualization complicate licensing so much?MID

Because software no longer sits still. Rules bind licenses to physical cores while VMs float across hosts, so vendors respond with worst-case rules: license everywhere the VM could run (Oracle soft partitioning), license all host cores for density (Microsoft), or demand continuous measurement for the discount (IBM sub-capacity). Topology data becomes license data.

SAM vs HAM vs ITAM?BASIC

ITAM is the umbrella discipline for all IT assets. HAM manages physical assets' lifecycle (procure→stock→deploy→dispose); SAM manages software rights and consumption. They share the CMDB and reinforce each other: you cannot do core-based licensing well without trustworthy hardware data.

What is multiplexing and why do interviewers love it?ADV

Putting middleware between users and a licensed backend (app server pooling 5 connections for 5,000 users). Vendors, notably Oracle and Microsoft, count the front-end humans/devices, not the pooled connections. It is loved because juniors answer "5 connections" and fail; the correct instinct is "count the people at the front".

What makes a license position "defensible"?MID

Every number traceable: consumption from named discovery sources with coverage KPIs, entitlements backed by documents (PO/invoice/agreement), rights application visible (License Workbench), timestamps fresh, and evidence archived (ILMT reports, isolation configs). Defensible means an auditor can attack the method and it holds.

Bank B · ServiceNow platform & SAM Pro (20)

SAM Foundations vs SAM Professional?BASIC

Foundations = manual tracking: models, entitlements, basic allocations. Pro adds the automation that makes SAM real: content-library normalization, the reconciliation engine, publisher packs, usage-based reclamation, SaaS management and cloud insights. Foundations records; Pro calculates.

Walk me through the data flow from a discovered install to the ELP.MID

Install lands in cmdb_sam_sw_install (via Discovery/SCCM/etc. through the MID Server) → its unique signature becomes a discovery model (cmdb_sam_sw_discovery_model) → normalization matches it to canonical publisher/product/version and maps to a software model (cmdb_software_product_model) → entitlements (samp_sw_entitlement) attach rights and a metric to that model → reconciliation applies metric math plus use rights → reconciliation result = the position.

Discovery model vs software model?BASIC

Discovery model: a unique raw software signature as seen by inventory ("MS SQL Svr 2019 Ent x64"), with a normalization status. Software model: the clean, licensable product record you manage entitlements against. Many discovery models map into one software model.

How does normalization work in SAM Pro?MID

Discovery models are matched against the ServiceNow Content Library (a curated catalog synced to the instance) on publisher/product/version/edition. Outcomes: fully normalized, partially, publisher-only, manually normalized via custom rules, or new/unrecognized. Reconciliation only trusts recognized software, so coverage (target >85–90%) is the gating KPI.

Normalization sits at 62%. What is the impact and your plan?MID

Impact: over a third of the estate is invisible to reconciliation, so any compliance dashboard is fiction. Plan: sort un-normalized discovery models by install count, manually map the top offenders (usually inhouse-packaged or renamed installers), submit missing titles to the content service, verify the library sync job runs, and re-measure weekly until >85–90%.

What is the Content Library / content service?BASIC

ServiceNow's centrally curated catalog of software products, suites and publisher content that instances sync on a schedule. It powers normalization and keeps pace with new versions; a broken sync quietly degrades coverage month after month.

Which tables would you actually open when debugging?ADV

cmdb_sam_sw_install for raw installs, cmdb_sam_sw_discovery_model for normalization status, cmdb_software_product_model for model mapping, samp_sw_entitlement for rights/metrics, plus the reconciliation results for engine output, and the CI (cmdb_ci_computer/VM) for cores and cluster links. Knowing the <table>.list trick reads as hands-on experience.

What role does the MID Server play for SAM?BASIC

It is the on-premises bridge: an application installed inside the customer network that executes Discovery probes and integration pulls (SCCM, ILMT, vCenter, Oracle DB queries) and pushes results to the cloud instance over outbound HTTPS. No MID Server, no on-prem data.

Which inventory sources can feed SAM Pro?BASIC

ServiceNow Discovery natively; connectors/integrations for SCCM/MECM, Intune, Jamf, ILMT/BigFix; vCenter for virtualization topology; SaaS APIs and SSO providers (Okta, Entra ID) for subscriptions; plus import sets for anything else. Multiple sources are normal; dedup and source-of-truth rules matter.

Explain the reconciliation engine's steps.MID

Per publisher: gather normalized installs/subscriptions and usage; join hardware and virtualization attributes; convert deployment to consumption via each entitlement's license metric (minimums, core factors, sub-capacity caps); apply use rights (downgrade/upgrade, suites, mobility); allocate owned entitlements optimally against consumption; publish per-model results with compliance status, true-up cost estimate, remediation options and reclamation candidates.

What is the License Workbench for?MID

It exposes how the engine reached a position: which entitlements were allocated to which consumption under which rights, drillable to install level. I use it to validate every Tier-1 ELP before publishing, to debug odd positions (usually data problems), and as the walkthrough surface when defending numbers to stakeholders or auditors.

What are remediation options?MID

Engine-generated proposals for closing a shortfall: reclaim unused installs, reallocate surplus entitlements, apply rights from other versions/editions, or purchase (with estimated cost). I work them cheapest-first and treat "buy" as the last resort, ideally bundled into a renewal.

How does license reclamation work end-to-end?BASIC

A reclamation rule defines the trigger (e.g. no usage in 90 days, from SCCM metering or SaaS activity); candidates are generated with evidence; a workflow notifies the user with a response window; on timeout or "no", an uninstall/unassign task executes (manually or automated) and the entitlement returns to the pool. Savings = reclaimed rights × unit cost, reported monthly.

How does SAM Pro integrate with software requests?MID

Catalog request triggers an entitlement check: if pool capacity exists, allocate and deploy with no spend; otherwise route through approval and procurement, which creates a new entitlement on fulfillment. This turns SAM proactive: compliance enforced at request time and reclaimed licenses actually get reused.

How does SAM Pro handle suites like Office or Creative Cloud?ADV

Suite definitions (content library or custom) declare component products. When components are detected on a device, the engine infers and counts the suite license instead of individual products, preventing double counting. I check suite logic whenever consumption looks inflated, e.g. a lone component consuming a full suite.

Why does SAM Pro need hardware and vCenter data, specifically?MID

Infrastructure metrics are hardware math: physical cores and sockets for Microsoft core licensing and Oracle processor calculations, cluster/host membership for mobility and soft-partitioning exposure, vCPU counts for IBM sub-capacity caps and cloud counting. Missing attributes silently break the calculators, so CI completeness is part of SAM QA.

What do publisher packs actually add?BASIC

Curated content and machinery per vendor: product definitions and suite logic, vendor-specific metrics and calculators (core minimums, core factors, PVU, sub-capacity), import tools (Microsoft MLS, IBM Passport Advantage), integrations (M365, ILMT, Oracle DB discovery) and dashboards. They encode the licensing rules so you configure data, not formulas.

How would you position SAM Pro against Flexera or Snow?MID

Point tools bring long-built inventory depth and publisher content; SAM Pro brings platform gravity: native CMDB, catalog/request workflows, ITSM integration and one data model, and it happily consumes third-party inventory anyway. If ServiceNow is already the backbone, SAM Pro usually wins on operationalization and TCO. I stay respectful of both; migrations between them are common consulting work.

What roles and access govern SAM Pro?BASIC

sam_admin for configuration (metrics, rules, connections, content) and sam_user for operating and reading. Practical governance: restrict entitlement editing tightly and audit changes, because ELP credibility dies if purchase records are casually editable.

A product's position looks obviously wrong. Debug it.ADV

In order: is the discovery model normalized and mapped to the right software model? Are installs deduplicated across sources? Does the entitlement carry the right metric, quantity, dates and SA/S&S flags? Are hardware/cluster attributes present on the CIs? Then open License Workbench and trace the allocation. Fix data, re-run reconciliation, never hand-edit results. Ninety percent of engine bugs are data bugs.

Bank C · Process & operations (15)

Design a SAM program from zero.MID

Assess maturity and data readiness → fix discovery coverage and CMDB attributes → collect entitlements (MLS/PPA/reseller reports + POs) → normalize to >85–90% → one Tier-1 publisher reconciled and validated end-to-end → quick reclamation win for credibility → operationalize (request checks, reclamation, renewal calendar, KPIs) → wave in remaining publishers.

What do you verify before committing implementation dates?MID

Discovery coverage vs estate (>95%), server hardware attributes and vCenter links present, CI dedup status, availability of 3–5 years purchase history and live agreements, usage metering availability, and stakeholder ownership. Every red item converts directly into timeline risk.

Why start with one publisher instead of all?BASIC

Depth builds trust; breadth builds dashboards nobody believes. One validated ELP with a savings story creates the political capital to fund the rest. Microsoft usually goes first: biggest spend, best data; unless Oracle/IBM audit risk dictates otherwise.

Where do entitlement records come from?MID

Publisher statements (Microsoft MLS/VLSC, IBM Passport Advantage, Oracle support/CSI records), reseller/LSP purchase reports, contracts and ordering documents, and internal PO/invoice archives. Cross-check at least two sources; every one of them has gaps.

Which KPIs would you put on a SAM scorecard?MID

Normalization coverage >90%, discovery coverage >95%, ELP freshness (<30 days for Tier-1s), savings realized YTD in currency (reclaim + avoidance + renewal reductions), % of requests fulfilled from the pool, and audit-evidence currency (e.g. ILMT reports archived quarterly).

Describe your operating cadence for a live SAM practice.BASIC

Daily: import/connector health and normalization exceptions. Weekly: reclamation queue, request approvals, new-software triage. Monthly: reconcile and validate Tier-1 publishers, publish compliance/savings. Quarterly: renewals pipeline, audit-evidence archiving, KPI review with stakeholders.

How do you prove SAM's value to a CFO?MID

One page, three currencies: risk removed (exposure quantified and closed), cash saved (reclamation and renewal reductions, YTD, auditable), and cost avoided (requests fulfilled from the pool instead of purchases). Never present license counts to finance; present money and trend lines.

How do you prepare a major renewal (EA, ULA, Passport Advantage)?MID

Start 9–12 months out: fresh validated ELP, usage trends per product, growth/reduction forecast with the business, walk-away and fallback options (right-size, alternatives, timing), and a negotiation pack for procurement with facts per SKU. Renewals are where the year's SAM work converts to money.

How does M&A affect SAM, and what would you do?ADV

Licenses are contracts: many are non-transferable or entity-scoped, ULAs may exclude acquired entities, agreements can require notification. Playbook: inventory both estates fast, freeze risky redeployments, review assignment clauses with legal, plan consolidation at the next renewal, and expect vendor audit interest around the transaction.

What is tricky about VDI licensing?ADV

Installs live on golden images and non-persistent sessions, so device counts mislead; user-based licensing usually fits better, and some products have explicit VDI terms. Roaming/secondary-use rights, RDS/Citrix CALs, and per-device rules in shared workstations all need explicit mapping before counting anything.

No usage data exists for a product. How do you optimize anyway?MID

Enable metering where possible (SCCM, SaaS APIs) and wait one cycle; meanwhile use proxies: last-login, assignment vs department function, version spread, and attestation campaigns ("confirm you still need X"). For SaaS, SSO logs are usually obtainable even when the vendor API is not.

Who are your stakeholders and what does each need?BASIC

Procurement/vendor management: ELPs and consumption forecasts for negotiations. Infrastructure/desktop: clean queues of reclamation and remediation tasks. Legal/compliance: audit posture and contract interpretations. Finance/CIO: savings, risk and budget forecasts. Security is a growing fifth: unsanctioned SaaS overlaps their shadow-IT agenda.

How do you stop non-compliant deployments from happening at all?MID

Move controls upstream: entitlement checks in the request catalog, change-gate review for anything touching Oracle/IBM/SQL clusters, architecture standards (dedicated Oracle clusters, SQL host licensing), and golden images without embedded licensable extras. Detection is good; prevention is cheaper.

How often should reconciliation run?BASIC

Scheduled nightly or weekly for steady state, plus on-demand after entitlement loads, big imports or architecture changes. What matters is the freshness SLA: no Tier-1 decision or report on a position older than ~30 days, and always check the last-run timestamp before quoting numbers.

An audit letter arrived this morning. Your first 48 hours?MID

Convene the war room (SAM, legal, procurement, sponsor), acknowledge without volunteering anything, pull the contract's audit clause, define single-channel communications, freeze relevant changes that could look like evidence tampering, and start our own measurement in SAM Pro so we know the truth before they do. Scope and ground rules get agreed in writing before any data collection.

Bank D · Microsoft (15)

State the Windows Server core licensing rules from memory.BASIC

License all physical cores; minimum 8 core licenses per processor and 16 per server; sold in 2-core packs; CALs still required. Standard grants 2 OSEs/VMs per full covering of the cores (stack it for more); Datacenter grants unlimited VMs on that host.

When does Datacenter beat Standard?BASIC

When VM density makes stacking Standard more expensive: Datacenter lists at roughly 5–6× Standard, so the crossover sits around 10–13 VMs per host. Below it, stack Standard; above it, or where density fluctuates, Datacenter.

Your options for licensing SQL Server in a VMware cluster?MID

Either per-VM (minimum 4 core licenses per VM; SA needed for the VMs to move freely under License Mobility) or license all physical cores of the hosts with Enterprise + SA for unlimited SQL VMs. Dense or mobile SQL estates favor host licensing on dedicated clusters; a few static VMs favor per-VM. The audit killer is per-VM licensing without SA on a DRS cluster.

Explain SQL Server failover rights.MID

With SA, a licensed primary earns free passive replicas: one HA, one DR, plus one in Azure. "Passive" is strict: no queries, no reports, no backup offloading; the moment a secondary serves reads it needs full licenses. Always inspect AlwaysOn configurations before certifying compliance.

Server+CAL vs per-core: how do you choose?MID

Server+CAL (SQL Standard) suits small, countable internal user populations. Per-core suits large or uncountable populations (web apps, external users) since it removes user counting entirely. Rough break-even: when CAL costs for your population exceed the core licenses, switch to per-core.

User CAL vs Device CAL?BASIC

User CAL covers one person on unlimited devices (default in laptop+phone workplaces). Device CAL covers one machine used by unlimited people (wins for shift workers on shared kiosks). External web users are better served by an External Connector or per-core server licensing.

What is the MLS and how do you use it?BASIC

The Microsoft License Statement: the consolidated volume-licensing purchase history including Software Assurance status, obtainable from Microsoft or the LSP. It seeds the entitlement side of a Microsoft ELP (SAM Pro's pack imports it directly), then gets cross-checked against internal POs because both sources have gaps.

Name the three M365 optimization plays.BASIC

Reclaim inactive seats (no sign-in 60–90 days: leavers, service accounts); right-size tiers (E5 assigned, E5-only features unused → E3/F3); eliminate overlap (standalone products already inside the bundle, or third-party tools duplicating bundled ones). All three run off the M365 connector's per-workload activity data.

How exactly do you justify an E5 → E3 downgrade?MID

Define what "using E5" means (Phone System, advanced security/compliance features, analytics), measure per-user activity on those workloads over ~90 days via the connector, exclude users required to hold E5 by policy (regulatory, security team), then downgrade the remainder and re-check next quarter. Evidence first, then the licensing change.

Which Software Assurance benefits matter most to SAM?MID

New version rights (upgrade coverage), License Mobility (virtualization and cloud movement), passive failover rights for SQL, and Azure Hybrid Benefit. SA status effectively decides whether an installation is compliant, so SA dates on entitlements are load-bearing data.

Explain Azure Hybrid Benefit.MID

Reusing owned Windows Server/SQL licenses with active SA (or subscriptions) in Azure, paying only base compute instead of license-included rates: commonly ~30–40% off those VMs. SAM's job: track which licenses are pledged to Azure so they are not double-counted on-prem, and feed migration business cases.

EA vs CSP: what changes for the SAM team?MID

EA: 3-year commitment, annual true-up, reductions only at renewal → SAM optimizes timing and true-up accuracy. CSP/NCE: monthly/annual flexibility but strict cancellation windows → SAM optimizes continuously and guards renewal auto-locks. Microsoft is pushing smaller EAs toward CSP/MCA-E, so consultants increasingly manage mixed estates.

What is SPLA and when does it appear?ADV

Services Provider License Agreement: monthly-reported licensing for providers hosting Microsoft software for third parties. It appears in outsourcing/MSP scenarios; the trap is customer workloads on provider infrastructure where nobody is clear whose licenses apply (SPLA vs customer's own with License Mobility).

How do dev/test environments get licensed?ADV

Via Visual Studio/MSDN subscriptions per developer, which cover dev/test use of most products, or free Developer editions (SQL). The audit classic is dev/test software serving production traffic, or non-subscribers using MSDN-sourced installs. Tag environments in the CMDB and reconcile dev/test separately.

Microsoft audit types and your response posture?MID

Soft "SAM engagement" (voluntary, vendor-funded, fishing for cloud migration leverage) vs formal audit under the contract clause (Big-4 executed). For the first: polite, controlled, no raw data without scoping, often declinable or convertible into a self-assessment. For the second: full audit-defense playbook, own numbers first from SAM Pro.

Bank E · Oracle (15)

Calculate: EE database on a 2×16-core Intel server. Licenses?BASIC

32 cores × 0.5 core factor = 16 Processor licenses, and the same count again for every option/pack used on that server. On IBM POWER the factor is 1.0, so the identical box would need 32.

What is the core factor table?BASIC

Oracle's published multiplier per chip family for Processor-metric licensing: x86 at 0.5, IBM POWER at 1.0, SPARC models between 0.25 and 0.75. Always check the current table per exact processor; and remember it does not apply in authorized public clouds.

Explain NUP and its minimums.BASIC

Named User Plus counts every human and device accessing the database, including through middleware (multiplexing does not reduce it). Minimums: 25 NUP per Processor for EE (after core-factor math) and 10 NUP per server for SE2. NUP is cheaper only for small user counts on small servers.

What are the SE2 rules that matter?MID

Licensed per occupied socket (Processor metric without core factor), maximum 2 sockets per server, a 16-thread runtime cap per instance, minimum 10 NUP per server on the user metric, and no RAC on current releases. SE2 on a 4-socket box is non-compliant regardless of usage.

How do database options get customers in trouble?MID

The binaries install with EE by default and features flip on easily: create one partitioned table, open an AWR report (Diagnostics Pack), enable compression. Each option then requires licensing at the full database's processor count. Detection is via DBA_FEATURE_USAGE_STATISTICS/V$OPTION, which both auditors and SAM Pro's Oracle discovery read.

What is DBA_FEATURE_USAGE_STATISTICS and its limits?ADV

An internal view recording feature usage with first/last-used timestamps and counters: the primary options-audit evidence. Limits: false positives exist (system-triggered usage, one-off accidents), so findings need verification and context ("used once by a DBA in 2021" is a negotiation point, not proof of ongoing use). Disable, document dates, argue materiality.

Hard vs soft partitioning?MID

Hard partitioning (accepted): physically capped CPU such as OVM with pinning, capped Solaris zones, IBM LPAR → license the capped cores only. Soft partitioning (not accepted): VMware, Hyper-V → Oracle's policy demands licensing everywhere the VM could run. Crucially that policy is a non-contractual document: aggressive in claims, negotiable in practice.

Your VMware strategy for Oracle workloads?ADV

Physically dedicated Oracle clusters, ideally a separate vCenter and storage boundary; host-affinity/DRS rules exported and archived as evidence; change-gate so no Oracle VM enters shared clusters; SAM review before any migration ticket closes. That converts an estate-wide policy claim into a small, defendable licensed island.

Explain a ULA and its exit.MID

Unlimited deployment of listed products for a fixed fee over 3–5 years. At expiry you either renew or certify: declare deployment counts that become your perpetual entitlements forever. Maximize legitimate deployment before exit, follow the certification clause exactly, and expect Oracle to sell renewal hard, sometimes with audit undertones.

The ULA cloud trap?ADV

Standard ULA terms limit or exclude counting public-cloud (AWS/Azure) deployments at certification even though you may run there during the term. Untreated, years of cloud growth certify to zero entitlements. Negotiate cloud counting into the ULA, or plan certifiable workloads on-prem until after exit.

Summarize Java SE licensing since 2019.MID

2019: commercial use moved to subscription (NUP/processor). January 2023: the SE Universal Subscription priced per employee: every employee and contractor counts whether they touch Java or not, tiered roughly $15 down to ~$5.25 per employee/month at list. Existing legacy subscriptions could renew, but new deals are per-employee.

A client gets an Oracle Java outreach email. Plan?MID

Treat it as pre-audit: no usage admissions, single point of contact. Internally: discover all JDK/JRE installs (SAM Pro finds them), identify what genuinely needs Oracle JDK, migrate the rest to OpenJDK builds (Temurin/Corretto/Zulu), document uninstall dates, then respond with the factual position. Oracle mines download logs, so assume they have IPs and dates.

How is Oracle licensed in AWS/Azure?ADV

Authorized-cloud policy: 2 vCPUs = 1 Processor license with hyper-threading on (1 vCPU = 1 without), and the core factor table does not apply. A 16-vCPU EC2 instance = 8 EE Processor licenses. SE2 counts differently (vCPU bands per "socket"). OCI gets friendlier treatment, which is a negotiation lever Oracle uses.

Why is cancelling Oracle support so hard?ADV

Support is ~22% of net license fees annually with uplifts, "matching service levels" forces one support level across a license set, and dropping support on unused licenses triggers repricing of the remainder toward list, often erasing the saving. Model repricing before terminating; sometimes third-party support or full termination of a set is the only clean cut.

Describe the Oracle audit process and one tactical rule.MID

GLAS issues notice under the contract (typically ~45 days), requests their collection scripts across the estate, then produces a findings report priced at list with policy-maximal interpretations. Tactical rule: never run scripts or return data before scope, entities, and data handling are agreed in writing, and have your own SAM Pro measurement done first so negotiations start from your numbers.

Bank F · IBM, distributed + mainframe (18)

What is a PVU?BASIC

Processor Value Unit: IBM's points-per-core currency, where the rate depends on the chip (commonly 70/core on 2-socket x86, 100–120 on bigger/Power boxes). Consumption = cores used × PVU rate; entitlements are bought in PVUs per product.

Full capacity vs sub-capacity?BASIC

Full capacity licenses every core in the physical server (or cluster) regardless of what the software uses; sub-capacity licenses only the VM/partition cores, capped at full capacity. Sub-capacity is routinely a 60–80% saving, granted only in exchange for continuous tool-based evidence.

The four sub-capacity eligibility conditions?MID

Eligible virtualization and products per IBM's lists; an approved measurement tool (ILMT or accepted alternative) deployed within 90 days of first sub-capacity deployment; reports generated at least quarterly; reports retained 2 years. Break any link and the affected estate reverts to full capacity, historically for the whole unproven period.

What is ILMT and how does it work?BASIC

IBM License Metric Tool: free, BigFix-agent based. Agents scan servers, detect IBM software via the software catalog, measure VM core consumption over time, and generate the official quarterly PVU sub-capacity reports that constitute audit evidence.

Can ServiceNow SAM Pro replace ILMT?MID

IBM has accepted certain third-party tools for sub-capacity reporting, including Flexera and ServiceNow SAM Pro, under conditions. The professional answer: it can, where IBM's current acceptance terms are met and ideally confirmed with IBM in writing for that customer; otherwise ILMT/BigFix remains the safe default, with SAM Pro consuming its data for the ELP.

An eligible host ran without an ILMT agent for a year. Consequence?MID

No evidence means no discount: that host is exposed to full-capacity licensing for the unproven period, plus back S&S, which is how IBM audit claims reach 3–10× expectations. Remediation: restore coverage immediately, attempt evidence reconstruction, and negotiate; prevention: a weekly coverage reconciliation of ILMT agents vs CMDB.

Who generates and keeps the quarterly reports?BASIC

The customer (or their SAM provider) generates them from ILMT at least quarterly and archives them for two years. Practical pattern: a calendar-driven task with sign-off, stored with the audit evidence pack; SAM consultants inherit this duty on managed engagements.

What are Cloud Paks and the VPC metric?MID

IBM's modern bundles (Cloud Pak for Data, Integration, AIOps…) licensed in Virtual Processor Cores: vCPU-based, container-aware entitlements covering multiple underlying products, with published ratios for converting legacy PVU entitlements. Model conversions against measured usage before accepting; conversion is a one-way commercial door.

How is containerized IBM software measured?ADV

The IBM License Service must run inside the OpenShift/Kubernetes cluster, tracking container vCPU consumption per product; its reports are the sub-capacity evidence for Cloud Paks. Deploy it before the first containerized IBM workload, same 90-day discipline as ILMT.

What is Passport Advantage?BASIC

IBM's volume licensing program and portal: entitlements, part numbers, renewals and the Subscription & Support (S&S) that carries version upgrade rights. Its entitlement report is the source document for the IBM side of an ELP; SAM Pro's IBM pack imports it.

Explain RVU and Authorized User in one breath each.ADV

RVU (Resource Value Unit): licenses by managed resources (endpoints, terabytes, activities) through product-specific tiered conversion tables. Authorized User: a named person per product/installation, where the same human may need multiple entitlements across environments depending on the product's definition. Both punish assumption; read the product's license information document.

IBM audit defense in three sentences?MID

The audit is won or lost before it starts: ILMT coverage complete, quarterly reports archived, classifications reviewed, entitlements reconciled continuously. During: control scope, verify the auditor's bundling/classification assumptions, and challenge full-capacity assertions with whatever evidence chain exists. Convert any settlement into committed spend the roadmap needed anyway.

MSU vs MIPS: which one matters for licensing?BASIC

MSU (Million Service Units) is the official capacity unit mainframe software charges are calculated on; MIPS is informal sizing shorthand (~8.5 MIPS ≈ 1 MSU as a rough rule). Contracts, SCRT reports and invoices all speak MSU, so SAM does too.

Explain R4HA and why it is the heart of mainframe cost.MID

MLC products bill on the Rolling 4-Hour Average of MSU consumption: the highest 4-hour average during the month, summed across LPARs where the product ran, sets that month's charge. Raw spikes are smoothed, installed capacity is irrelevant, and every mainframe cost lever (capping, batch scheduling, product isolation, zIIP offload) exists purely to lower that one number.

SCRT vs ILMT: compare them in four lines.MID

SCRT is z/OS, ILMT is distributed. SCRT reports monthly to IBM; ILMT reports quarterly and is retained internally for two years. SCRT measures R4HA MSU peaks per product per LPAR; ILMT measures peak vCPU allocation per VM. And the consequence differs: SCRT output directly sets the monthly invoice, while ILMT evidence preserves a discount that audits test retroactively.

MLC vs IPLA on the mainframe?MID

MLC (z/OS, CICS, IMS, Db2 for z/OS, MQ): recurring monthly charges scaled to measured MSUs via curves like AWLC. IPLA on z: one-time perpetual purchase plus annual S&S, sized in Value Units converted from machine MSUs by a tier table. One machine carries both regimes, so an IBM Z ELP has a monthly half and a classic entitlement half.

Name four levers to reduce mainframe software cost.ADV

Soft capping via defined/group capacity so WLM enforces a bill ceiling; moving batch out of the online peak so 4-hour averages never stack; isolating chargeable products onto fewer LPARs (charged where they run); and zIIP offload, since specialty-engine MSUs escape the software meter. Bonus lever five: renegotiating the ISV stack (BMC, Broadcom, Rocket), often the fastest saving on the box.

What is Tailored Fit Pricing and when would you recommend it?ADV

IBM's consumption-based contracts for Z (2019+): pay for MSUs actually consumed against a negotiated baseline instead of managing R4HA peaks, with variants for enterprise consumption and new/dev-test workloads. Recommend for growing, spiky or modernizing estates tired of capping games; stay on AWLC + caps when peaks are already engineered low. Always replay 12 months of SCRT data under both models before deciding.

Bank G · SaaS, cloud & other publishers (14)

How do you discover SaaS without installers to scan?BASIC

Identity and money instead of file systems: direct API connectors to major vendors (M365, Salesforce, Adobe, Zoom…), SSO sign-in data from Okta/Entra ID for usage, and spend detection on expense/AP data to surface shadow IT the connectors never see.

Direct connector vs SSO data: strengths of each?MID

Direct connectors give seat assignments, tiers and feature-level usage (deep but only for supported vendors). SSO gives last-login breadth across hundreds of apps (shallow but universal). Mature practice: connectors for the top-spend apps, SSO as the wide net, spend data as the tripwire for the unknown.

What is software spend detection / shadow IT discovery?BASIC

Matching corporate expense and accounts-payable records against a catalog of tens of thousands of known SaaS vendors, surfacing purchases that never passed through IT. It finds the departmental credit-card subscriptions that duplicate sanctioned tools and carry the security risk.

Your top SaaS optimization plays?BASIC

Reclaim inactive seats on 30/60/90-day rules; downgrade over-provisioned tiers; consolidate overlapping apps (three meeting tools → one); catch renewal auto-locks with a calendar and usage evidence. Benchmark: 25–30% of SaaS spend is typically recoverable in year one.

What is BYOL and what must SAM track for it?MID

Bring Your Own License: reusing owned licenses on cloud infrastructure (Azure Hybrid Benefit, Oracle on authorized clouds, License Mobility). SAM must track which entitlements are pledged to cloud so they are not simultaneously counted on-prem, and verify eligibility conditions (SA active, authorized environment).

How does licensing differ between on-prem and IaaS for the big publishers?MID

Cloud replaces physical-core math with vCPU rules: Oracle counts 2 vCPU = 1 processor without core factor; Microsoft offers license-included pricing or AHB/mobility for BYOL with per-vCPU minimums; IBM measures VPC/PVU on vCPUs with the same evidence obligations. The recurring theme: check each publisher's cloud policy, never assume on-prem rules travel.

Why is a renewal calendar a SAM artifact and not just procurement's?BASIC

Because the leverage is usage evidence with lead time: reductions and tier changes need data assembled 3–6 months before auto-renewal locks in. SAM owns the evidence; procurement owns the negotiation; the calendar binds them.

A department insists their SaaS tool is "fully used". Verify how?BASIC

Pull assignment vs activity from the connector or SSO logs: active users in 30/60/90 days, feature-tier usage if available, and cost per active user vs the alternative. Then decide together with the data on the table; SAM referees with evidence, not opinions.

What is SAP Digital Access and how do you advise on it?ADV

SAP's answer to indirect access: when third-party systems (webshops, CRMs, bots) create SAP documents through interface accounts, you license the nine defined document types per document created instead of claiming named users for every human behind the integration. Advice pattern: measure document creation, model document-based vs legacy named-user cost, and adopt via a negotiated conversion at renewal, never under audit pressure.

What changed with VMware under Broadcom, and what should SAM do?MID

Perpetual licenses ended; everything is per-core subscription bundled into VCF or VVF, with minimums (16 cores per CPU, 72 per order) and renewal quotes running multiples of legacy pricing. SAM's response: exact core inventory per cluster, edition mapping (VVF where the full VCF stack isn't used), early renewal negotiation with alternatives priced, and treating the estate as a strategic cost program, not a routine renewal.

How does token licensing work for engineering software?ADV

Vendors like PTC, Siemens and Ansys sell a shared token pool; launching a feature draws tokens, metered by a license server (FlexLM-style). Compliance evidence and optimization both live in the license-server logs: measure true peak concurrency, size the pool to a high percentile rather than the absolute peak, and watch denial events as both a productivity and negotiation signal.

How do SAM and FinOps relate?MID

Different meters, same mission: unit-cost control. FinOps owns cloud consumption (reservations, rightsizing, showback); SAM owns licenses, compliance and vendor contracts. They overlap on BYOL (AHB, Oracle's vCPU rule), SaaS spend and marketplace purchases, so mature organizations run them as sister practices sharing data; BYOL decisions made by either alone are usually wrong.

Marketplace and credit-card software buying: governance answer?MID

Cloud-marketplace and expense-card purchases bypass procurement, so SAM meets them with detection (spend/AP mining, SSO discovery), a paved road (catalog request with entitlement checks that is easier than the credit card), and policy with teeth at renewal. The goal is capture, not prohibition; engineering will always buy something first.

Is open-source software in SAM scope?MID

Yes, twice over. Commercially: "free" software with paid tiers or relicensing history (Oracle JDK, Docker Desktop, Redis/Elastic) creates real invoices. Legally: copyleft obligations (GPL) matter for distributed products. Minimum practice: inventory OSS like paid software, track the risky titles' license changes, and coordinate with engineering's SBOM/security tooling.

Bank H · Scenarios, ELP drills & behavioral (14)

Walk me through building a Microsoft ELP with no SAM tool at all.ADV

Entitlements: MLS from the LSP cross-checked with POs. That log is what makes it defensible.

Re-establish the single-channel rule through the sponsor: all auditor contact via the named lead, with a short brief explaining why (every stray statement becomes evidence). You discover the customer massively over-bought before you arrived. The buyer is in the room.ADV

Frame it as market movement, not error: "requirements and licensing models changed; today's data shows surplus we can monetize." Focus forward: reharvest, renewal reductions, conversion credits. The goal is the saving, not the blame; humiliating the person who signs your change requests is bad consulting.

DBAs refuse credentials for Oracle discovery. Now what?MID

Understand the real objection (security, workload, fear of findings) and de-risk it: read-only account, reviewed query list, agreed windows, DBA-run scripts as an alternative where they execute and hand over outputs. Escalate as a risk statement if blocked: "without measurement, the Oracle position is unknown and audit exposure is unquantified." Data access is a governance decision, not a personal favor.

Procurement doesn't trust the SAM dashboard. Rebuild confidence.MID

Find one number they know is wrong and autopsy it together in License Workbench down to the install: usually a normalization gap or a stale entitlement, then fix the class of error, not the instance. Publish the coverage KPIs next to every dashboard so consumers see data quality explicitly. Trust returns through one transparent post-mortem, not through assurances.

A publisher offers a "free license review." Recommendation?BASIC

Decline politely or control it tightly: vendor-funded reviews are sales and audit reconnaissance. Counter-offer: "we run our position internally and will discuss the renewal with our own numbers." If contractually pressured, treat it as a formal audit: scope, NDA, single channel.

Six publishers, a team of two. Prioritize.MID

Score each on spend × audit aggression × contract events in the next 12 months (renewals, ULA expiry). Typical result: Microsoft (spend + renewal) and Oracle or IBM (audit risk) first; Adobe/VMware/SAP staged behind. Publish the rationale so "why isn't X covered" has an answer, and automate the covered ones before widening.

Explain PVU sub-capacity to a CFO in 30 seconds.BASIC

"IBM charges by engine size. Full capacity means paying for the whole ship's engines even if our software uses one deck. Sub-capacity means paying only for the deck we use, but IBM demands a certified meter running at all times. The meter costs discipline; skipping it multiplies the bill retroactively. Our job is keeping the meter healthy."

Customer wants to skip normalization and "just load entitlements first."MID

Explain the dependency visually: entitlements answer "what we own", but without normalization there is no trustworthy "what we use", so reconciliation will compare purchases against noise. Compromise: load entitlements in parallel (it is slow work anyway) while normalization runs to its gate, but hold any published position until coverage passes ~85–90%.

You disagree with the vendor's licensing interpretation. Process?ADV

Contract text first: the ordering document and program terms outrank policy PDFs and blog posts. Document our reading, gather precedent, involve legal for material amounts, and negotiate commercially where genuine ambiguity remains, trading certainty for consideration. Never silently adopt the vendor's worst-case reading; never rely on a friendly rep's email either.

Tell me about a SAM mistake you made and what changed.MID

Pattern for a strong answer: a specific early error (trusting a dashboard without checking normalization; quoting an ELP with a stale data cut), the concrete consequence (procurement negotiated on wrong numbers), the fix (coverage gates and freshness stamps on every report), and the habit that persists today. Interviewers are testing for data humility; give them evidence you have it.

11.3 Beyond the big three: publisher pocket guides

Eight more publishers you will meet. One model, one trap, one move each:

🟨SAP

Model: named-user classes (Professional, Limited…) + engine/package metrics (orders, revenue, cores) + annual LAW/USMM self-measurement. Trap: Digital Access: third-party systems creating SAP documents. Move: classify users by actual activity before the yearly measurement; model Digital Access both ways (document licenses vs legacy named-user claims).

🟩VMware by Broadcom

Model: subscription only, per core, bundled into VCF (full stack) or VVF; minimums of 16 cores per CPU and 72 cores per order. Trap: renewal shock, 3–10× legacy pricing. Move: per-cluster edition mapping (don't buy VCF where VVF suffices), inventory cores precisely, negotiate hard (25–40% off openers is common), and price the exit alternatives.

🔴Adobe

Model: named-user Creative Cloud/Acrobat via VIP (transactional) or ETLA (3-yr enterprise). Trap: shared logins breaking named-user terms; Sign/Stock consumption overages. Move: SSO-based activity reclamation, 60-day idle rule; true-down at ETLA anniversary.

🟦Citrix

Model: per-user/per-device universal subscriptions (legacy concurrent still around). Trap: concurrent-to-user migrations misconverted. Move: meter real concurrency before renewal; hybrid entitlements need one counting rule, written down.

☁️Salesforce

Model: edition per user + add-on sprawl (API calls, sandboxes, Data/Einstein). Trap: integration users consuming full licenses; unused full-copy sandboxes. Move: license-type right-sizing (platform vs full CRM), API-user consolidation, sandbox pruning at renewal.

🎩Red Hat

Model: all subscription: RHEL per socket-pair/vCPU bands, OpenShift per core. Trap: "we cancelled but kept running it" (subscription lapse ≠ uninstall) and unregistered dev estates. Move: match subscriptions to running instances quarterly; watch embedded RHEL inside appliances.

📐Autodesk

Model: named-user subscriptions only (network/perpetual retired) + Flex tokens for occasional users. Trap: shared accounts violating named-user terms after the concurrent era. Move: usage-based mix of subscriptions vs Flex; reclaim at 90-day idle.

🧪Engineering tools (PTC, Siemens, Ansys…)

Model: token/credit pools drawn by feature, metered by license servers (FlexLM/FlexNet). Trap: peak-window denials and token exhaustion counted as compliance events. Move: license-server log analytics for true peak concurrency; buy tokens to the 95th percentile, not the peak.

11.6 Glossary (interview-speed definitions)

AHB (Azure Hybrid Benefit)
Reuse Windows/SQL licenses with SA in Azure at compute-only rates.
BYOL
Bring Your Own License: applying owned licenses to cloud infrastructure under eligibility rules.
CAL
Client Access License: per-user or per-device right to access a Microsoft server product.
CI / CMDB
Configuration Item in the Configuration Management Database: the platform's inventory of hardware, VMs and their relationships.
Content Library
ServiceNow's curated software catalog powering normalization, synced to the instance on schedule.
Core factor
Oracle's per-chip multiplier converting physical cores to Processor licenses (x86 = 0.5).
CSP / NCE
Microsoft's partner-led subscription channel and its New Commerce Experience rules.
Discovery model
Unique raw software signature detected by inventory, carrying a normalization status.
Downgrade / upgrade rights
Newer licenses covering older versions; older licenses covering newer only with active maintenance.
EA
Microsoft Enterprise Agreement: 3-year volume agreement with annual true-up.
ELP
Effective License Position: entitlements minus consumption after use rights. The deliverable.
Entitlement
Recorded proof of purchased rights: quantity, metric, dates, evidence.
GLAS
Oracle's audit organization (formerly LMS).
Hard / soft partitioning
Oracle's split of virtualization tech into accepted (capped) and not accepted (VMware) for limiting licenses.
IASP
IBM Authorized SAM Provider: program trading mature tool-based practice for audit relief.
ILMT
IBM License Metric Tool: the free agent-based meter that earns sub-capacity licensing.
ISO 19770
ITAM standards family: -1 processes, -2 SWID tags, -3 entitlement schema.
License metric
The unit a publisher counts: user, device, core, processor, PVU, NUP, employee…
License Mobility
SA benefit allowing server licenses to move across hosts/farms/clouds.
License Workbench
SAM Pro's validation surface showing how entitlements were allocated to consumption.
CMP
Country Multiplex Pricing: all IBM Z machines in a country priced as one pool.
Digital Access (SAP)
Licensing third-party-created SAP documents per document instead of indirect named users.
IFL
Linux-only mainframe engine; excluded from z/OS MLC charges, ILMT rules apply on top.
LPAR
Logical partition of a mainframe: the "VM" of IBM Z, unit of MLC measurement.
MID Server
On-network bridge application executing discovery and integrations for the cloud instance.
MLS
Microsoft License Statement: consolidated volume-licensing purchase history.
Multiplexing
Middleware pooling connections; vendors still count the front-end users.
Normalization
Matching raw install strings to canonical publisher/product/edition/version.
NUP
Oracle Named User Plus: named users/devices with per-processor minimums.
Passport Advantage / PoE / S&S
IBM's licensing program, Proof of Entitlement, and Subscription & Support (upgrade rights).
Publisher pack
SAM Pro add-on encoding one vendor's products, metrics, imports and dashboards.
PVU
Processor Value Unit: IBM's points-per-core metric.
Reclamation
Harvesting unused licenses via usage rules and workflows for reuse.
Reconciliation
The engine run comparing entitlements to metered consumption, producing positions.
Remediation options
Engine-proposed fixes for shortfalls: reclaim, reallocate, apply rights, buy.
RVTools
Free VMware inventory exporter; the manual-ELP standard for hosts, cores and clusters.
SA
Software Assurance: Microsoft maintenance carrying upgrade rights, mobility, failover, AHB.
Shelfware
Licenses bought but never deployed or used.
Software model
The licensable product record entitlements attach to.
SPLA
Microsoft's monthly-reported licensing for service providers.
Sub-capacity
Licensing VM/partition cores instead of full hosts, in exchange for tool evidence.
SWID tag
ISO 19770-2 identification tag installed with software for precise recognition.
True-up
Periodic payment reporting deployment growth under an agreement.
ULA
Oracle Unlimited License Agreement: fixed-fee unlimited deployment ending in certification or renewal.
VPC
Virtual Processor Core: IBM's container-aware vCPU metric for Cloud Paks.
MIPS / MSU
Mainframe capacity: MIPS is informal sizing; MSU (Million Service Units) is what software charges are based on.
MLC
Monthly License Charge: recurring mainframe software pricing (z/OS, CICS, Db2 for z/OS…), scaled to measured MSUs.
R4HA
Rolling 4-Hour Average of MSU usage; its monthly peak sets each MLC product's bill.
SCRT
Sub-Capacity Reporting Tool: monthly mainframe usage report submitted to IBM; the z-side ILMT, but it directly drives the invoice.
TFP
Tailored Fit Pricing: IBM Z consumption-based contracts replacing R4HA peak management.
Token licensing
Engineering-software model: a shared token pool drawn per feature use, metered by license servers (FlexLM).
VU (Value Unit)
IBM's tiered MSU-to-unit conversion for one-time-charge (IPLA) mainframe products.
zIIP
Mainframe specialty engine for eligible work (Db2, Java); its MSUs don't count toward MLC.
